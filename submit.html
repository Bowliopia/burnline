Submit to Burnline

We're looking for fiction and artwork that pulses with cyberpunk energy. Burnline is a new publication, run by people who love the genre and want to see it evolve. If you've created something bold, strange, gritty, or just plain cool—send it our way.

Fiction Submissions

We pay $50 USD per accepted piece. It’s our goal to grow Burnline into a publication that can pay a competitive per-word rate—but we’re not quite there yet. We want to be transparent with contributors while we build something worth supporting.

Word count: 500 – 10,000 words

Submit by: Emailing your story as a .doc, .docx, or .pdf to submissions@burnline.net

No AI-generated or AI-assisted work will be accepted. We only want human-created fiction—your voice, your ideas, your originality.

What We’re Looking For

We’re excited about cyberpunk in all its forms—classic and modern, urban and off-world, sleek and gritty. Some elements we tend to love:

  • Near-future worlds with corporate control or state surveillance
  • Characters altered by technology—implants, drugs, code, or otherwise
  • Decaying cities, rogue AIs, neon-drenched atmospheres
  • Resistance, rebellion, hackers, grief, and dreams

That said, genre is a slippery thing. If your piece is strange, experimental, or sits on the edge of cyberpunk, send it anyway. If it moves us, we’ll publish it.

Art Submissions

We’re also accepting cyberpunk-themed visual art for digital publication. This includes illustration, digital collage, animation stills, photography, and glitch art—anything that contributes to a speculative aesthetic grounded in mood, message, or madness.

Submit by: Emailing submissions@burnline.net with a link or attachment of your work (JPG, PNG, or a link to your portfolio or file share).

No AI-generated or AI-assisted work will be accepted.

A Note on Cover Letters

Emails and cover letters are far less important to us than your fiction or artwork. If a part of your personal story contributes to what you've made, we’d love to hear about it—but otherwise, keep it simple. We're here for the work itself.
