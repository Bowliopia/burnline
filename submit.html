Submit to Burnline

Burnline is a new publication run by a couple of people who love the cyberpunk genre and want to see it evolve. We're currently open for short fiction and art submissions. If you've created something cyberpunk and would like to get it published, send it our way.

Fiction Guidelines Art Guidelines

Fiction Guidelines

We’re excited about cyberpunk in all its forms—classic and modern, urban and off-world, sleek and gritty. In fiction, we're looking for what you'd expect; near-future worlds with corporate control, people altered by tech-implants, you get it. Above all however, we care about character. We want people who make us feel something while they're navigating whatever wild setting you’ve built. Human connection is what makes good cyberpunk work. If the content moves us, we’ll usually publish it.

We're less interested in anything outside of the cyberpunk subgenre for this magazine. If you're submitting, please make sure you'd classify your work as cyberpunk specifically.

Word Limit 500 – 10,000 words
Pay Rate $50 USD per accepted piece. It’s our goal to eventually pay a competitive per-word rate, but we’re not quite there yet.
How to Submit Email your story as a .doc, .docx, or .pdf to contact@burnline.net. Please include the word "Submission" in the subject line.

No AI-generated or AI-assisted work will be accepted. We only want human-created fiction. There's irony somewhere in here.

Art Guidelines

We’re accepting cyberpunk-themed visual art for digital publication. This includes illustration, digital collage, photography, animation stills, glitch art, or anything else that fits the tone of Burnline. We care more about the vibe than the medium.

How to Submit: Email contact@burnline.net with your artwork attached (JPG or PNG) or linked via portfolio or file share. Please include the word "Submission" in the subject line.

No AI-generated or AI-assisted work will be accepted.

A Note on Cover Letters

Emails and cover letters are much less important to us than your fiction or artwork. If a part of your personal story contributes to what you've made, we’d love to hear about it—but otherwise, keep it simple. We're here for the work itself.
